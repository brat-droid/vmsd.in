Module 7: Advertising & Campaign Planning

Topic 3: Campaign Lifecycle

A marketing campaign typically follows a structured lifecycle, from initial planning to post-campaign analysis. Understanding these stages helps in efficient execution and continuous improvement.

Stages of a Marketing Campaign Lifecycle:

  • 1. Planning:
    • Define objectives (SMART goals).
    • Identify target audience.
    • Conduct market research.
    • Develop budget and timeline.
    • Brainstorm creative concepts and messaging.
  • 2. Development & Creation:
    • Create ad copy, visuals, and other marketing assets.
    • Develop landing pages or specific campaign destinations.
    • Set up tracking and analytics.
  • 3. Launch & Execution:
    • Deploy campaigns across selected channels.
    • Monitor initial performance and make real-time adjustments.
  • 4. Monitoring & Optimization:
    • Continuously track KPIs (Key Performance Indicators).
    • Perform A/B testing to optimize elements like headlines, CTAs, and visuals.
    • Adjust bidding strategies, targeting, or ad placements as needed.
  • 5. Analysis & Reporting:
    • Gather all campaign data.
    • Analyze performance against initial objectives.
    • Identify successes, failures, and key learnings.
    • Generate comprehensive reports for stakeholders.
  • 6. Post-Campaign Review & Learnings:
    • Document insights for future campaigns.
    • Refine strategies based on what worked and what didn't.

Each stage is interconnected, and a systematic approach ensures maximum effectiveness and learning from every campaign.
