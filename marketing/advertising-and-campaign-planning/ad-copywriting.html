Module 7: Advertising & Campaign Planning

Topic 1: Ad Copywriting

Ad copywriting is the art and science of writing text for advertisements and other marketing materials. Its primary goal is to persuade a target audience to take a specific action, such as making a purchase, signing up for a newsletter, or visiting a website.

Principles of Effective Ad Copywriting:

  • Know Your Audience: Understand their needs, desires, pain points, and language.
  • Focus on Benefits, Not Just Features: Explain how the product/service solves a problem or improves their life, rather than just listing what it does.
  • Strong Headline: Grab attention immediately. It should be clear, concise, and compelling.
  • Clear Call to Action (CTA): Tell the reader exactly what you want them to do next (e.g., "Buy Now," "Learn More," "Sign Up").
  • Conciseness: Every word should count. Eliminate jargon and unnecessary phrases.
  • Emotional Appeal: Connect with the audience's emotions to create a memorable and persuasive message.
  • Urgency/Scarcity: Encourage immediate action by highlighting limited-time offers or limited availability.
  • Credibility: Use testimonials, statistics, or guarantees to build trust.

Great ad copy is persuasive, memorable, and drives results.
