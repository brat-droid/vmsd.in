Module 10: Growth Hacking & Virality

Topic 1: Viral Loops

A viral loop is a mechanism that encourages existing users to invite new users, who then invite more new users, creating a self-sustaining cycle of growth. It's a core concept in growth hacking, aiming for exponential user acquisition.

The effectiveness of a viral loop is often measured by the viral coefficient (K-factor), which indicates how many new users each existing user generates.

Components of a Viral Loop:

  • Exposure: Existing users are exposed to a mechanism that encourages sharing (e.g., a share button, a referral prompt).
  • Invitation: Users send invitations or share content with their network.
  • Conversion: New users receive the invitation and convert into active users.
  • Value Proposition: The product or service offers clear value to both the inviter and the invited, motivating participation.
  • Incentive (Optional but common): Rewards for both the referrer and the referee (e.g., discounts, premium features).

Examples of Successful Viral Loops:

  • Dropbox: Offered extra storage space to users who referred new sign-ups.
  • Hotmail: Included a simple "P.S. Get your free email at Hotmail" signature in every outgoing email.
  • Facebook: Relied on social connections and the desire to connect with friends.

Designing an effective viral loop requires understanding user psychology, providing clear incentives, and making the sharing process as seamless as possible.
