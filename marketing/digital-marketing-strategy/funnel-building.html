Module 6: Digital Marketing Strategy

Topic 4: Funnel Building

A marketing funnel (also known as a sales funnel or conversion funnel) is a visual representation of the journey a potential customer takes from initial awareness of a brand or product to making a purchase and beyond.

Understanding and optimizing your marketing funnel is crucial for converting prospects into loyal customers.

Stages of a Typical Marketing Funnel:

  • Awareness: The top of the funnel, where potential customers first become aware of your brand, product, or service. (e.g., social media ads, blog posts, SEO).
  • Interest: Prospects show interest in your offerings and seek more information. (e.g., website visits, content downloads, email sign-ups).
  • Consideration: Prospects evaluate your solution against competitors and consider making a purchase. (e.g., product demos, case studies, webinars).
  • Conversion: The point where a prospect becomes a customer by making a purchase or taking a desired action. (e.g., online purchase, form submission, sign-up).
  • Retention/Loyalty: Post-purchase activities aimed at keeping customers engaged, encouraging repeat business, and turning them into advocates. (e.g., customer support, loyalty programs, exclusive content).

Each stage of the funnel requires different marketing tactics and content to guide the customer effectively towards conversion.
