Module 3: Branding and Positioning

Topic 2: Positioning Maps

Positioning maps (also known as perceptual maps) are visual representations of how consumers perceive different brands or products within a market. They are powerful tools for understanding competitive landscapes and identifying opportunities for differentiation.

How Positioning Maps Work:

  • Typically, a positioning map is a two-dimensional graph with axes representing key attributes or dimensions that are important to consumers (e.g., price vs. quality, innovation vs. tradition).
  • Brands are plotted on the map based on consumer perceptions of where they stand on these attributes.
  • The closer two brands are on the map, the more similar they are perceived to be by consumers.
  • Gaps in the map can indicate untapped market opportunities where a new product or brand could successfully position itself.

By creating and analyzing positioning maps, marketers can gain valuable insights into consumer perceptions, identify their brand's current position, and strategize for a desired future position.
