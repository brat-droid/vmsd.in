Module 4: Market Research and Segmentation

Topic 3: Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning (STP)

The STP (Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning) model is a core concept in marketing that helps businesses identify the most valuable segments of their market and develop products and marketing messages tailored to them.

1. Segmentation:

Dividing a broad consumer or business market into sub-groups of consumers (segments) based on some type of shared characteristics.

  • Geographic: Region, city size, climate.
  • Demographic: Age, gender, income, education, family size, occupation.
  • Psychographic: Lifestyle, personality, values, attitudes, interests.
  • Behavioral: Usage rate, loyalty, benefits sought, readiness to buy.

2. Targeting:

Evaluating each market segment's attractiveness and selecting one or more segments to enter. This involves assessing segment size, growth potential, structural attractiveness, and company objectives/resources.

3. Positioning:

Arranging for a product to occupy a clear, distinctive, and desirable place relative to competing products in the minds of target consumers. This is where the value proposition comes into play.

The STP model is a powerful framework for developing effective marketing strategies by focusing resources on the most promising customer segments.
