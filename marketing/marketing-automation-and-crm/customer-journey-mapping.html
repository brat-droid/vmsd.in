Topic 3: Customer Journey Mapping

Customer journey mapping is the process of creating a visual representation of the entire experience a customer has with your company, from initial awareness to post-purchase interactions. It helps businesses understand customer motivations, pain points, and opportunities for improvement at each touchpoint.

Key Steps in Customer Journey Mapping:

  • 1. Define Scope & Objectives: What specific customer journey are you mapping? What insights do you hope to gain?
  • 2. Identify Buyer Personas: Focus on the journey of a specific persona (as discussed in Module 4).
  • 3. List Touchpoints: Identify all the interactions a customer has with your brand (online, offline, direct, indirect).
  • 4. Map the Journey Stages: Typically includes Awareness, Consideration, Purchase, Retention, and Advocacy.
  • 5. Document Customer Actions, Thoughts, & Emotions: For each stage and touchpoint, consider what the customer is doing, thinking, and feeling.
  • 6. Identify Pain Points & Opportunities: Where are customers struggling? Where can you improve their experience?
  • 7. Create the Visual Map: Use flowcharts, timelines, or other visual aids to represent the journey.
  • 8. Share & Act: Distribute the map to relevant teams and use it to inform strategic decisions and improvements.

Customer journey mapping provides a holistic view of the customer experience, enabling businesses to design more effective and customer-centric marketing strategies.
