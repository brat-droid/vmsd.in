Topic 2: Decision-making Models

Consumer decision-making is not always a straightforward process. Various models have been developed to explain the stages consumers go through when making a purchase.

Common Decision-Making Models:

  • The Five-Stage Model:
    1. Need Recognition
    2. Information Search
    3. Evaluation of Alternatives
    4. Purchase Decision
    5. Post-purchase Behavior
  • The Howard-Sheth Model: A more complex model that incorporates psychological variables like motives, attitudes, and perceptions, and how they interact with external stimuli.
  • The Engel-Kollat-Blackwell (EKB) Model: Similar to Howard-Sheth, but with a stronger emphasis on information processing and external influences.

These models help marketers identify intervention points and tailor their strategies to influence consumers at each stage of their decision journey.
