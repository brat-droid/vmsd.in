Module 2: Consumer Psychology

Topic 1: Understanding Consumer Behavior

Consumer behavior is the study of how individuals, groups, or organizations select, buy, use, and dispose of ideas, goods, and services to satisfy their needs and wants. It involves understanding the various factors that influence these decisions.

Factors Influencing Consumer Behavior:

  • Cultural Factors: Culture, subculture, and social class significantly impact consumer choices.
  • Social Factors: Reference groups, family, and social roles and status play a crucial role.
  • Personal Factors: Age, life-cycle stage, occupation, economic situation, lifestyle, personality, and self-concept.
  • Psychological Factors: Motivation, perception, learning, beliefs, and attitudes.

By analyzing these factors, marketers can gain insights into consumer preferences, purchasing habits, and responses to marketing stimuli.
