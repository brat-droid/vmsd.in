Make Inferences About Your Data with Confidence

Hypothesis testing is a statistical method that is used to make inferences about your data. In this section, we will learn how to formulate and test hypotheses.

Null and Alternative Hypotheses

The first step in hypothesis testing is to formulate a null hypothesis and an alternative hypothesis. The null hypothesis is a statement that there is no effect, while the alternative hypothesis is a statement that there is an effect.

P-values

The p-value is the probability of obtaining a result as extreme as the one that was observed, assuming that the null hypothesis is true. A small p-value indicates that the null hypothesis is unlikely to be true.

Type I and Type II Errors

There are two types of errors that you can make in hypothesis testing:

  • Type I error: Rejecting the null hypothesis when it is actually true.
  • Type II error: Failing to reject the null hypothesis when it is actually false.
