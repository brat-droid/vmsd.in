Design Process & Workflow

The design process, whether for a new store, a renovation, or a specific visual merchandising campaign, is a structured approach that ensures a cohesive and effective outcome. It typically involves several distinct phases, from initial conceptualization to final implementation and evaluation.

Phase 1: Discovery & Research

  • Client Briefing: Understanding the client's vision, brand identity, target audience, budget, timeline, and specific objectives.
  • Market Research: Analyzing current retail trends, competitor strategies, and consumer behavior.
  • Site Analysis: For physical spaces, assessing the location, existing architecture, foot traffic, and any constraints or opportunities.
  • Audience Understanding: Deep diving into the demographics, psychographics, and shopping habits of the target customer.

Phase 2: Concept Development

  • Brainstorming & Ideation: Generating creative ideas that align with the brief and research findings.
  • Mood Boards: Creating visual collages of images, colors, textures, and materials to convey the desired aesthetic and atmosphere.
  • Sketches & Schematics: Developing initial hand-drawn or digital sketches of layouts, displays, and key design elements.
  • Concept Presentation: Presenting initial ideas to the client for feedback and approval.

Phase 3: Design Development

  • Space Planning: Developing detailed floor plans, traffic flow diagrams, and zoning strategies.
  • Fixture & Furniture Selection: Choosing specific display units, furniture, and other elements that fit the concept and budget.
  • Material & Finish Specification: Selecting all surfaces, paints, flooring, and other materials.
  • Lighting Design: Planning the type, placement, and intensity of lighting to highlight products and create ambiance.
  • Graphic & Signage Design: Developing all in-store communication, including promotional signs, wayfinding, and branding elements.
  • 3D Renderings & Walkthroughs: Creating realistic visualizations of the proposed design to help the client envision the final space.

Phase 4: Documentation & Production

  • Technical Drawings: Producing detailed architectural and construction drawings for contractors and fabricators.
  • Specifications: Documenting all materials, finishes, fixtures, and equipment.
  • Budget & Timeline Refinement: Finalizing costs and project schedules.
  • Procurement: Ordering and managing the delivery of all necessary components.

Phase 5: Implementation & Evaluation

  • Construction & Installation: Overseeing the physical build-out and setup of the store or display.
  • Visual Merchandising Setup: Arranging products, dressing mannequins, and fine-tuning displays.
  • Opening & Launch: The grand unveiling of the new space or campaign.
  • Post-Launch Evaluation: Assessing the success of the design against initial objectives, gathering feedback, and analyzing sales data. This iterative process allows for continuous improvement.

This structured workflow ensures that design projects are executed efficiently, creatively, and strategically, leading to impactful retail environments that resonate with customers and achieve business goals.
