Sector Analysis

Sector analysis is the process of evaluating the economic and financial conditions of a specific sector of the economy. This article explains why it's a crucial part of investment research.

What Are Market Sectors?

The stock market is typically divided into a number of broad sectors, which group together companies that are in similar businesses. The Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS) defines 11 sectors, including:

  • Information Technology
  • Health Care
  • Financials
  • Consumer Discretionary
  • Communication Services
  • Industrials
  • Consumer Staples
  • Energy
  • Utilities
  • Real Estate
  • Materials

Why is Sector Analysis Important?

A company's performance is heavily influenced by the health of its sector. A rising tide lifts all boats, and a strong sector can boost the performance of even mediocre companies within it. Conversely, a struggling sector can be a headwind for even the best-run companies. Sector analysis helps investors identify which parts of the economy are growing and which are facing challenges. This top-down approach can help narrow the universe of potential investments.

Analyzing a Sector

Analyzing a sector involves looking at its growth prospects, the level of competition, the regulatory environment, and its sensitivity to business cycles. For example, the Consumer Staples sector tends to be defensive and performs well during economic downturns, while the Consumer Discretionary sector is cyclical and performs well during economic expansions.
