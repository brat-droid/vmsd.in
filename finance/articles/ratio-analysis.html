Ratio Analysis

Ratio analysis involves calculating and comparing financial ratios from a company's financial statements to gain insights into its performance. This article covers key categories of financial ratios.

What is Ratio Analysis?

Financial ratios are mathematical comparisons of financial statement accounts or categories. These ratios allow investors to get a quick and standardized look at a company's financial health and performance. Ratios are most useful when compared against a company's own historical performance or against the ratios of its competitors in the same industry.

Key Categories of Financial Ratios

  • Liquidity Ratios: These measure a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations. The Current Ratio (Current Assets / Current Liabilities) is a common example.
  • Profitability Ratios: These measure a company's ability to generate profits from its sales or assets. The Net Profit Margin (Net Income / Revenue) is a key profitability ratio.
  • Leverage Ratios: These measure the extent to which a company is using debt to finance its assets. The Debt-to-Equity Ratio (Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity) is a widely used leverage ratio.
  • Valuation Ratios: These help investors assess whether a stock is overvalued or undervalued. The Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio (Stock Price per Share / Earnings per Share) is the most famous valuation ratio.

Putting Ratios in Context

A single ratio in isolation provides limited information. The true power of ratio analysis comes from trend analysis (how a ratio changes over time) and industry analysis (how a company's ratios compare to industry averages). This comparative analysis helps to paint a much clearer picture of a company's strengths and weaknesses.
