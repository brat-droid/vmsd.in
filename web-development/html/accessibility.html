Accessibility

Web accessibility is the practice of designing and developing websites that can be used by everyone, including people with disabilities. This article covers the importance of accessibility and how to use ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) labels and other techniques to create an inclusive web experience.

Why is Accessibility Important?

An accessible website is one that can be used by people with a wide range of abilities and disabilities. This includes people who are blind or visually impaired, deaf or hard of hearing, and those with motor or cognitive impairments. By making your website accessible, you are not only complying with legal requirements in many countries, but you are also creating a better experience for all users.

Example: ARIA Label

<button aria-label="Close">X</button>

ARIA Labels and Roles

ARIA is a set of attributes that can be added to HTML elements to improve their accessibility. This article will cover how to use ARIA roles, states, and properties to provide additional information to assistive technologies like screen readers. We will also explore other accessibility best practices, such as providing alternative text for images and ensuring proper keyboard navigation.
